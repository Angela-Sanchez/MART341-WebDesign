Egg and Dairy-Free Birthday Cake

This nondairy and egg-free vanilla cake recipe, adapted from Cake by Courtney, is the perfect alternative for those with allergies.
The neutral color for both the cake and rich, buttercream frosting from The Conscious Plant Kitchen make it an ideal solution for a birthday cake or cupcakes that can be customized.


INGREDIENTS
3 1/2 cups cake flour
2 cups granulated sugar
2 teaspoons baking soda
1 teaspoon salt
2 cups almond milk or other non-dairy milk at room temperature
4 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
2/3 cup light oil
2 tablespoons white distilled vinegar


FOR THE FROSTING
1 cup unsalted, softened nondairy butter
3 cups powdered sugar
1 teaspoon vanilla extract
2-4 tablespoons nondairy milk


INSTRUCTIONS


TO MAKE THE CAKE
  1. Preheat your oven to 350 degrees F. Spray three 8-inch or four 6-inch pans with non-stick spray, line the bottoms of each pan with parchment rounds, and spray again. Optionally, you can use a lined or sprayed nonstick cupcake pan. Set aside.

    2. Pictured here is the Wilton 6-inch round pan set that I prefer. It makes the perfect child-sized birthday cake.



  2. In a large bowl, combine the almond milk, vanilla, oil and vinegar. Set aside.

  3. In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment (or large bowl if using a hand mixer), combine the cake flour, sugar, baking soda, and salt. Using a sifter is optional but can assist with breaking up lumps and creating a fluffier cake. Stir to combine.



  4. With the mixer on low speed or with a hand mixer, add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients. Stir just until incorporated. Scrape down the sides and bottom of the bowl and mix again on low for another 10 to 20 seconds.



  5. Divide the batter evenly between the pans. Bake for 22 to 25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center of the cake comes out with just a few moist crumbs on it.



  6. Let cool 10 minutes in the pans before inverting onto wire racks to cool completely.

  7. If needed, level each cake layer with a cake leveler. To store the cake layers, wrap each cake layer in plastic wrap after it has completely cooled. The cake layers can be stored in the freezer for up to a week with two layers of plastic wrap. To store longer, wrap the layers in an additional layer of foil and place in a zip-lock bag.


TO MAKE THE FROSTING

  1. In a medium bowl, cream soft nondairy butter with an electric hand mixer until smooth, fluffy and whiter in color for about 30 seconds.

  2. Add the powdered sugar, vanilla, and 1 tablespoon of nondairy milk.

  3. Beat on low speed with an electric hand mixer. This prevents the powdered sugar from bursting out of the bowl. Then, increase speed to medium-high speed moving around the bowl to bring all ingredients together.



  4. Beat until a smooth, spreadable frosting forms. Don't overbeat.

  5. If frosting becomes too thick, beat in more milk, adding a few drops at a time. If frosting becomes too thin, beat in a small amount of extra powdered sugar.
